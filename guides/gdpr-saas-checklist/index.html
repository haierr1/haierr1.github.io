GDPR checklist for SaaS — what you actually need before launch

Not legal advice — a builder's checklist. GDPR work for a small SaaS is 90% predictable: the same six areas, the same documents, the same banner everyone postpones. Here's the list, in launch order.

1. Know your data (30 min, do it first)

  • List every category of personal data you touch: account data, billing, usage/events, logs/IP, support emails.
  • For each: why you process it (this becomes your lawful basis) and how long you keep it (retention).
  • List your processors: hosting, payments, email, analytics, support tooling.

2. Lawful bases (Art. 6) — pick, don't improvise

  • Contract — providing and billing the service itself.
  • Legitimate interests — security, fraud prevention, product improvement (do the balancing note).
  • Consent — non-essential cookies, analytics, marketing. Withdrawable at any time.
  • Legal obligation — tax/accounting retention.

3. The documents

  • Privacy policy structured around: controller identity, data categories, lawful bases, retention, processors, transfers, user rights, complaint route to a supervisory authority.
  • Cookie policy with an actual cookie table.
  • DPAs signed with every processor (all major providers have standard ones).
  • EU/EEA hosting or SCCs + supplementary measures for transfers.

4. Cookie consent that actually complies

  • Non-essential scripts must NOT run before opt-in — a banner that only informs is non-compliant.
  • Reject must be as easy as accept. Granular categories. Choice persists and is revocable ("manage cookies" link).

The standard trick: ship gated scripts as type="text/plain" data-consent="analytics" and re-insert them as real scripts only on consent. We open-sourced a ~170-line dependency-free implementation: gdpr-cookie-banner.

5. User rights — be ready before the first email

  • Access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection — respond within a month.
  • For a small SaaS: a documented manual process is fine. Know how you'd export and how you'd delete.

6. Ongoing hygiene

  • Breach process: detect → assess → notify authority within 72h if risky.
  • Records of processing (Art. 30) — a spreadsheet is acceptable at small scale.
  • Revisit the list every time you add a processor or data category.
